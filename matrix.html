WHAT: Investigating a Challenge
Make a visual representation of climate change impact and add TL labels.
Research local recycling schemes and co-write a class manifesto in TL.
Prototype a sustainable product and label it in TL.
Create a bilingual info poster on global pollution or deforestation.
Gather data on school recycling habits and present findings in TL graphs.
Compare renewable energy sources and create an infographic in TL.
WHO: Stories of Action
Create a new sustainable fashion brand story in TL with logo and slogan.
Interview a climate enthusiast and record a TL podcast.
Investigate sustainable design innovators and create a bilingual showcase.
Research an eco-activist, local or global, and describe their work in TL.
Use numbers to illustrate an eco-hero’s effect and add TL captions.
Study an eco-changemaker and create a TL poster highlighting their achievements.
WHY: Imagining Green Futures
Create artwork showing “my future job for the planet” with TL captions.
Discuss how all careers can be ‘green careers’ and describe your future eco-role in TL.
Research eco-design jobs or inventions and create a bilingual mini-portfolio.
Predict positive future environmental changes and describe what makes a ‘green city’ in TL.
Use numerical data to compare how different jobs affect climate change and explain your findings in TL.
Explore what climate researchers do and create a TL explanation of why their work matters.
HOW: Everyday Choices
Create “A day in my eco-life” collage with TL captions.
Create a school eco pledge and multilingual posters for corridors.
Build a recycled-object prototype and add TL labels.
Track, write, and compare short TL diary entries on personal eco-habits.
Estimate your school’s energy use and describe possible improvements in TL.
Investigate how everyday choices (recycling, saving water, walking to school) make a difference and create a bilingual presentation.
